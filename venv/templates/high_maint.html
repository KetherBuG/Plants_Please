High Maintenance Plants

1. Bonsai Tree

While the bonsai tree may appear elegant, they are extremely difficult to take care of. There are many different specimens for bonsai trees, so you need to be aware of their needs and which climate they are best suited for. Bonsai trees are meant to live outdoors and require careful pruning of roots and shape. Luckily, they never outgrow their pot and are a cute and elegant addition to your home.

2. Pointsettia

Commonly known as the holiday plant you see while walking down a Christmas-decorated store aisle, pointsettias require a lot of care and special placing in order to bloom to its fullest beaty. There is a specific period that pointsettias can rebloom but even a small ray of light could disrupt this process. Even if it does rebloom properly, it is best to leave it to professionals to bring you home a newly trimmed pot of these beautiful flowers. However, these flowers are a lovely addition to your living room along with your christmas tree and stockings. Drop by a local shop every year for a new pot of this beautiful, jolly plant and take in the added spirit in your own home.

3. Easter Lily

Another holiday plant, this delicate flower is also a plant designated for the outdoors. It requires cold weather in order to bloom well and prepare for rebloom (bring indoors during this period). After the flower has bloomed, it must be brought outdoors and planted while the weather gets warmer. However, beware as this plant withers slowly indoors and can also bloom again in late summer.

4. Moth Orchid

While very elegant, these flowers require a lot of patience as they take longer to bloom flowers. This is because much energy is required to produce flowers, so the orchid must lose all of its old flowers to grow new ones. Luckily, you will see flowers bloom 2 to 3 times a year. There are many tips online on taking care of this plant, especially in order to ensure your house can be home to these beautiful flowers every 4 to 6 months.
