Low Maintenance Plants

1. Chinese Evergreen


The Chinese Evergreen also known as Aglaonema, is a plant native to Asia and New Guinea. This plant thrives in low light (do not place directly under sun) and can be maintained with an irregular watering schedule (do not water too much). They start off small (fitting for an office plant) but eventually become quite large (floor plants) but this will take time as their growth rate is quite slow. Need a mild level of humidity (if they turn brown it is too dry). DO NOT INGEST (POISONOUS IF EATEN)!
You can buy the plant at Home Depot for a mere $20: Home Depot Chinese Evergreen

2. Snake Plant


The Snake Plant, also known as the Dracaena trifasciata or mother-in-law’s tongue, is native to West Africa. It is known to bring good luck and block out negative “chi”.Similar to the Chinese Evergreen plant it thrives in indirect sunlight, however, do not put a lot of water and use free draining soil (rots easily). The Snake Plant also cleans the air well (emits oxygen and cleans the air from toxins).
You can buy the plant at Home Depot for a mere $20: Home Depot Snake Plant

3. Jade Plant (aka the Money Plant)


The Jade Plant, also known as the Crassula Argentea, is known for bringing luck and money ($$)! This plant originally came from South Africa but was largely cultivated in Europe and America. This plant requires at least four hours of sunlight but will suffice if kept under an indirect sunlight (probably by the window). You only have to water it when the soil is dry (could be per week or per month but brown = more water needed).
You can buy the plant at Home Depot for a mere $25: Home Depot Jade Plant

4. Maidenhair Fern


The maidenhair fern is one of 250 ferns in the subfamily of ferns called Vittarioideae of the family Pteridaceae. The Greek, meaning is "unwetted", referring to the ferns' ability to shed water without becoming wet. Ferns don’t need much light because they’re used to growing on forest floors, completely shaded by a canopy of trees, and will be happy in a spot offering indirect light for at least part of the day. They demand high humidity and like to be watered and misted frequently. While this may seem like more work than the rest, the outcome is a plant with delicate, lacy leaves.
You can buy the plant from Amazon for a mere $15: Amazon Maidenhair Fern

5. Aloe Vera Succulent


This succulent is the only one we will be going in depth about! This succulent is very easy to take care of and will need to be watered every three weeks (about an 1 to 2 inches deep) and prefers indirect sunlight. There are many more benefits to this little succulent aside from being a pretty decoration. It can also be used to be used for medicinal purposes such as sunburns, reducing inflammation on your face and wounds, and promoting hair growth For more benefits look at this website: Aloe Vera Benefits (Medical News Today)
You can buy the succulent at Walmart for less than $4: Walmart Aloe Vera Succulent
